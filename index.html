Project Overview

A comprehensive data analysis project demonstrating the complete data analytics process from Ask to Act, with actionable marketing recommendations for a bike-share company.

Business Question

How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

Data Source

Q1 2019 & Q1 2020 Divvy trip data from Chicago's bike-share system

Goal

Design marketing strategies to convert casual riders into annual members

Key Findings

Data-driven insights that drive strategic decisions

Ride Duration

Casual Riders 36 minutes avg
Annual Members 12 minutes avg

Casual riders take 3x longer rides, indicating leisure-focused usage vs. functional commuting.

Weekly Patterns

Casual Weekend Usage High spikes
Member Weekday Usage Consistent

Clear weekend preference for casual riders vs. steady weekday commuting for members.

Strategic Recommendations

Actionable strategies to convert casual riders

Weekend Warrior Membership

Create a discounted weekend-focused membership tier targeting casual riders' primary usage pattern.

Expected: 15-25% conversion

Commuter Benefits

Market cost-effectiveness and convenience of annual membership for daily commuting.

Expected: 200-300% ROI

Tiered Memberships

Develop flexible, lower-priced membership tiers as low-commitment entry points.

Expected: 30-40% uptake
