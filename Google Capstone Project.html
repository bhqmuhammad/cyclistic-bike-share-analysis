Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

Navigating Speedy Success: A Strategy to Convert Casual Riders into Annual Members

Presented to: Lily Moreno & The Cyclistic Executive Team

Date: June 8, 2025

1. The Ask: The Business Task

The director of marketing, Lily Moreno, has identified a key business opportunity: maximizing the number of annual memberships to ensure future growth. The company's financial analysts have determined that annual members are significantly more profitable than casual riders.

Therefore, the central business task is to:

Design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members.

To achieve this, we must first answer the foundational question:

"How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?"

2. Prepare: Data Sources

Our analysis is grounded in Cyclistic's historical trip data. Due to the scope of this initial analysis, we focused on two key datasets, as recommended for this case study:

  • Divvy Trips 2019 Q1: Provides a pre-pandemic baseline of rider behavior.
  • Divvy Trips 2020 Q1: Offers insight into the beginning of a shift in usage patterns.

Data Characteristics & Limitations:

  • The data is public and made available by Motivate International Inc., serving as a proxy for Cyclistic's fictional data.
  • It is reliable, relevant, original, comprehensive, and current (ROCCC).
  • Critically, data privacy regulations prohibit access to Personally Identifiable Information (PII). This means we cannot directly link pass purchases to credit card numbers to track individual casual riders over time.

3. Process: Data Cleaning & Manipulation

To ensure the data was accurate and ready for analysis, several data processing steps were performed using spreadsheet software. This documentation ensures our process is transparent and repeatable.

  • Data Aggregation: The `Divvy_Trips_2019_Q1` and `Divvy_Trips_2020_Q1` datasets were merged into a single master file for comprehensive analysis.
  • Feature Engineering: Two new columns were created to facilitate analysis:
    • `ride_length`: Calculated by subtracting `started_at` from `ended_at` to determine the duration of each trip.
    • `day_of_week`: Calculated using the `WEEKDAY` function to determine the day on which each ride started (1=Sunday, 7=Saturday).
  • Data Integrity Checks: The combined dataset was cleaned by filtering out trips with a negative or zero `ride_length`. This removes bad data entries and trips where bikes were returned almost immediately, which could skew the analysis.

4. Analyze & Share: Key Findings

Finding 1: Ride Duration

The most significant difference between the two user groups is the average length of their rides.

Casual riders take substantially longer trips compared to annual members. This strongly suggests different primary use cases.

  • Members: Likely use bikes for shorter, utilitarian trips like daily commutes.
  • Casual Riders: Likely use bikes for leisure, recreation, and tourism.

4. Analyze & Share: Key Findings

Finding 2: Weekly Usage Patterns

The number of rides per day reveals a clear divergence in weekly habits.

Member rides peak during the work week, while casual rides spike on weekends.

  • Members: High, consistent usage from Monday to Friday confirms the commute hypothesis.
  • Casual Riders: A dramatic increase in rides on Saturdays and Sundays points directly to weekend leisure activities.

5. Act: Top 3 Recommendations

Based on our analysis, we can confidently say that casual riders and members use our service differently. The following recommendations are designed to leverage these insights to convert casual riders into profitable members.

1. Create a "Weekend Warrior" Membership.

Target the high weekend usage of casual riders with a discounted membership tier valid only on Saturdays and Sundays. This provides a low-barrier entry point, showing the value of a membership for their specific use case.

2. Launch a "Ride Longer for Less" Campaign.

Run a digital marketing campaign highlighting the cost-effectiveness of memberships for longer, leisurely rides. Use messaging like, "Love exploring Chicago on two wheels? An annual pass pays for itself after just a few weekend adventures."

3. Introduce a "Commuter Trial" Promotion.

Target existing casual riders via email and in-app notifications with a time-sensitive offer, such as a "5-Day Commuter Pass" for the price of a single day pass. This encourages them to experience the convenience of Cyclistic for work travel, a behavior strongly correlated with annual membership.
