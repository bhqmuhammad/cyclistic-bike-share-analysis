How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

A data-driven marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members

Business Scenario

The Challenge

Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago, wants to maximize the number of annual memberships. The director of marketing believes converting casual riders to annual members is key to future growth.

Key Question

"How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?"

Key Finding #1

Casual riders take 3x longer rides than annual members

Annual Members

~12 minutes

Average ride duration suggests functional, commute-focused usage

Casual Riders

~36 minutes

Average ride duration indicates leisurely, recreational usage

Insight: Different usage patterns suggest different value propositions for each group.

Key Finding #2

Usage patterns differ significantly by day of week

Annual Members

  • Higher usage on weekdays
  • Peak times: morning & evening commute
  • Consistent usage pattern

Casual Riders

  • Massive spike on weekends
  • Saturday & Sunday peak usage
  • Recreational activity pattern

Insight: Members use bikes for work, casual riders for leisure.

Marketing Recommendations

1

Launch "Weekend Warrior" Membership

Create a discounted membership tier specifically for weekend riders, appealing directly to casual riders' primary usage pattern.

Target: Casual riders who primarily use bikes on weekends for recreational activities

2

Market Commuter Benefits

Highlight cost-effectiveness and convenience of annual membership for daily commuting through targeted weekday campaigns.

Strategy: Compare daily costs vs. annual membership savings for regular commuters

3

Introduce Tiered Memberships

Develop flexible, lower-priced membership options with set monthly rides as low-commitment entry points.

Approach: Bridge the gap between casual usage and full annual commitment
